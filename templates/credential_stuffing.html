Credential stuffing is one type of most common cyberattacks in the world. Attackers use breached credentials to log into different websites and see if they can get access to the user accounts.

The reason why credential stuffing works is that many people re-uses same username or email and passwords for different websites. And In case the credentials are stolen through a data breach or a phishing attack. The attacker may have a chace to get access to the victim's account on a different website by the same account. Credential stuffing is one of the brute force attacks, which has 0.1% of success rate. You may think the chance is not that high, But remember, there are probably billions of people uses internet these days. By doing some simple math, millions of people could be attacked.

For instance, if a user uses the same login credentials for Facebook and PayPal and the login credentials for Facebook was leaked. Then the attacker and get access to his/her paypal account, which will affect the safety of the user's assets.
